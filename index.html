About Me

I am a detail-oriented and adaptable professional with 15+ years of customer service and communications experience combined with growing expertise in front-end web development, website management, data analytics, and Salesforce administration.

My interest in coding, specifically web development, began when I was only 15. While everyone in my high school computer literacy class was learning what a computer was, I was making myself and my friends our own websites through one of the earliest CMS made available online, FreeWebSpace (Notice the page notifies of Webs.com retirement). They started in 2000, and where quickly rebranded into FreeWebs in 2001, although the original freewebspace.net interface was available through late 2006. They were later rebranded again to Webs.com in 2008, again allowing freewebs.com domains to remain existant for some time. In 2011, VistaPrint bought the company. Over the next decade, they rewrote the code and enchanced it. And finally, in 2021, Webs was retired and VistaPrint offered a Vista x WIX service in its place. Wix started in 2006 in Tel Aviv, Israel, and is still going strong today. It is a great service, but it just isn't the same as the original FreeWebSpace.

I was sad to see it go down. Even if it wasn't the greatest software to use, I was there when the ancient magics were written (Yes, that is a C.S. Lewis reference).

PS: There is a FreeWebSpace website at freewebspace.com. It is not the original FreeWebSpace, but it appears to be a replica of the original with an updated copyright date. I don't know much about it, but I assume someone snagged it up sometime after its retirement and brought it back to life. I don't believe it is the original, but rather a nice homage to the service that started it all for me.

Projects

Rest In Piecez gaming clan website and social hub

As one of my earlier projects, I made a static website for my Halo based gaming clan, Rest In Piecez. This website included individual player profiles, displayed on one page, in a column, on their own respective cards before content cards where a best practice. The site also included a highlights page with embedded videos, gaming clips, and images, as well as a Records page to keep track of clan scrimages. This site was deleted in 2011 when FreeWebs became Webs.com.

CJC Chat - a social hub for job corps students

In 2008, I was enrolled in Clearfield Job Corp in Clearfield, Utah. At this campus, internet access was extremely limited. All known social media sites for the time (pretty much just Facebook and MySpace) were blocked. As it was a time when cell phones were not yet a neccessity, I began hearing students 'wishing for a way to communicate with/find their friends.' We all just ran around campus looking for each other. So I searched for a solution and found Spruz. I built Clearfield Job Corps it's own internal social media hub. Students could customize their profiles, make posts, comment on posts, send messages, and play little flash games. Now, if your friend was in their dorm, you could just send a message from your dorm's PCs to let them know you were looking for them. This site ran from 2008 to 2012, with a concurrent 1200 users (maxed at 3618 users), with myself as the administrator. The site was cjcchat.spruz.com. I fell away from it with my busy life, and the site inevitably dwindled out on it's own.
