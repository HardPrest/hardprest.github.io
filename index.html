CJC Chat - a social hub for job corps students

In 2008, I was enrolled in Clearfield Job Corp in Clearfield, Utah. At this campus, internet access was extremely limited. All known social media sites for the time (pretty much just Facebook and MySpace) were blocked. As it was a time when cell phones were not yet a neccessity, I began hearing students 'wishing for a way to communicate with/find their friends.' We all just ran around campus looking for each other. So I searched for a solution and found Spruz. I built Clearfield Job Corps it's own internal social media hub. Students could customize their profiles, make posts, comment on posts, send messages, and play little flash games. Now, if your friend was in their dorm, you could just send a message from your dorm's PCs to let them know you were looking for them. This site ran from 2008 to 2012, with a concurrent 1200 users (maxed at 3618 users), with myself as the administrator. The site was cjcchat.spruz.com. I fell away from it with my busy life, and the site inevitably dwindled out on it's own.
